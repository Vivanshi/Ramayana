 The Ramayana written by Tulsidas known as Ramcharitmanas, is a sixteenth century epic. The story revolves around Rama, who was the seventh avatar of the Hindu God Vishnu. He is the central figure of the Hindu epic Ramayana, which is the principal narration of the events connected to His incammation on earth. His ideals and His greatness. He was the eldest son of King Dashratha- the ruler of Kosala kingdom. Rama's life and journey is one of the adherence to dharma despite harsh tests and obstacles and many pains of life and time. The adherence made him earn the name of Maryada Purushottam which literally means The Perfect Man or Lord of self-Control or Lord of Virtue. For the sake of his father's honour. Rama abandons his claim to Ayodhya's throne to serve an exile of fourteen years in the forest. His wife Sita and brother Lakshmana decide to join him, and all three spend the fourteen years in exile together. While in exile, sita is kidnapped by Ravana, the king of Lanka. After a long and aruduous search. Rama fights a colossal war against Ravana's armies. In a war of powerfull and magical beings, greatly distructive weapony and battles, Rama slays Ravana in battle and liberates his wife. Having completed his exile, Rama returns to be crowned king in Ayodhya and eventually becomes the emperor. The whole story is devided into seven parts known as 'Kands.'
